Reflection on "The Playlist"

The first episode of the series talks about Daniel Ek, the creator of Spotify. He believes that the music industry is about to undergo a digital revolution,
and he wants to lead that transformation. He applied to Google but was declined because he didn't have a university degree. Daniel had an idea to create
a streaming service for free music, but people in the industry were not used to the idea. They believed that he needed to get a radio license and rights
from record labels to play music. Despite this, Daniel continued pitching his idea and approached Per Sundin, the CEO of the Sony Sweden music branch.
However, Per was not interested in the idea and warned him against it. Daniel realized that the youth of today could be the key to popularizing his idea.
So, he went to the local school to spread the word about Spotify. My thoughts on Daniel are that he is an amazing coder; he even hacked Google and
made an online marketing website where companies get ten percent of the revenue for every click on their page. He is also a person who has a very
innovative mindset and an entrepreneurial spirit, and because of that, he was able to make a significant impact on the music industry, making Spotify an
app used by millions of users all over the world. I think that the storyline of the series was really interesting, and as the show went on, it really showed
how revolutionary it was. The storyline really showed how many struggles Daniel Ek faced just to make Spotify; it showed that even with the countless
rejections of his idea for a streaming service, he still pursued it and never gave up on it. Overall, The Playlist is a solid and well-written series, with good
acting and a solid premise.
